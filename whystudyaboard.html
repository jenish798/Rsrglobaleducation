Why Study Aboard

Why Study Abroad

Explore the top reasons why studying abroad can be a life-changing experience!

Expanded Career Opportunities

According to the Institute of International Education (IIE), 74% of students who studied abroad reported a significant improvement in their employability. Studying abroad equips students with cross-cultural competencies, a global network, and the adaptability required in today's diverse job markets. Employers actively seek candidates who can demonstrate global perspectives and skills gained through international exposure.

Cultural Awareness and Global Perspectives

Research from The Open Doors Report shows that students who study abroad develop a deeper understanding of different cultures, languages, and global issues. This cross-cultural awareness fosters an open-minded approach and enhances problem-solving abilities, making students more effective in multicultural environments.

Enhanced Academic Performance

A study conducted by The Journal of International Students highlights that students who have studied abroad perform better academically after returning home. They show increased critical thinking skills, higher levels of independence, and stronger academic motivation. This enhanced mindset often leads to a more rigorous and impactful learning experience.

Personal Growth and Self-Confidence

A 2017 study by the American Institute For Foreign Study (AIFS) found that 80% of study-abroad participants reported increased self-confidence. Navigating life in a foreign country challenges students to step outside their comfort zones, boosting resilience, problem-solving, and self-reliance—all of which contribute to personal growth.

Networking and Global Connections

Overseas education offers students access to an extensive network of international peers, professors, and industry professionals. According to Global Education Research (2022), 67% of students reported building valuable professional networks during their time abroad, which helped in landing internships, mentorships, and jobs post-graduation.

Language Proficiency

For those learning a second language, immersion in an international environment is one of the most effective ways to master a language. A report from The British Council states that immersion increases language fluency more rapidly compared to traditional classroom settings. Students studying in non-English-speaking countries, for example, acquire language skills that are highly valuable in the global job market.

Increased Global Mobility

According to UNESCO, studying abroad improves international mobility, with 60% of students citing it as a stepping stone to live and work in another country. Studying abroad provides exposure to different work cultures and environments, creating avenues for international job opportunities and a broader sense of professional mobility.

Broader Range of Study Programs and Specializations

Studying abroad allows students to access a wider variety of academic programs, research opportunities, and specialized fields of study that may not be available in their home countries. Research by QS World University Rankings highlights that top universities abroad often offer cutting-edge research programs and diverse subject offerings, particularly in emerging fields like artificial intelligence, renewable energy, and biotechnology.

Competitive Edge in Graduate School Applications

Admissions committees for top graduate programs, such as those at Harvard, Oxford, and Stanford, often view international experience as an advantage. According to The Graduate Management Admission Council (GMAC), students with study-abroad experience tend to score higher on interviews and applications due to their proven ability to handle challenges in diverse settings and adapt to unfamiliar environments.

Lifelong Friendships and Cross-Cultural Bonds

A Global Student Survey conducted by World Education Services (WES) reveals that 77% of students who study abroad develop long-lasting friendships with peers from around the world. These relationships can lead to enriching collaborations, whether in the workplace, academia, or through personal connections that transcend borders.
