Poland

Why Poland?

Poland is a premier study destination offering high-quality education, affordable tuition, and a low cost of living. Renowned universities provide English-taught programs across various disciplines. Its strategic location in Europe, rich cultural heritage, and dynamic student life enhances the academic experience. Additionally, international students benefit from part-time work opportunities and promising post-graduation prospects.

Cost of Studying in Poland

Living Cost

€400–€800/month {₹36,000 to ₹72,000} (including rent, food, and transport)

Tuition Fees

€2,000–€6,000/year {₹1,80,000 to ₹5,40,000}

Medicine & MBA: €8,000–€15,000 {₹7,20,000 to ₹13,50,000}

Poland Eligibility Requirements
Academic Requirements
  • Bachelor’s: 12th grade from a recognized board, IELTS (if required).
  • Master’s: Relevant Bachelor's Degree, IELTS.
Visa Requirements
  • University admission letter.
  • Proof of funds (₹8–10 lakh+ for expenses).
  • Valid passport & health insurance.
  • Visa application & interview (if required).

Cost of Studying in Poland

To apply for studies in Poland, students need to submit a completed application form, academic transcripts (10th, 12th, and Bachelor's if applicable), a valid passport, and proof of English proficiency (IELTS/TOEFL if required). Additional documents include a Statement of Purpose (SOP), Letters of Recommendation (LORs), proof of funds, and health insurance. Some universities may require an entrance exam or interview.
